From waste to learning

We supply schools with boxes of otherwise unwanted produce for their food and health course, directly from local farms.

Why is this important?

Food Waste

⅓ of all food is wasted/thrown out/goes in the bin


Food production is responsible for about 25 percent of the world's greenhouse gas emissions


Of the global freshwater usage, 70 percent is used for agriculture


School

Just 4 out of 10 teachers have the expertise to teach the “Food and health”-course


Lack of knowledge about nutrition and a healthy lifestyle affects public health greatly


Education levels impact knowledge of nutrition and a healthy lifestyle and therefore educational programs from an early age are necessary to combat social inequalities


Sustainability

If everyone in the world were to consume as much as every Norwegian, we would need 3,6 planet Earth’s


To limit the effects of climate change, there needs to be a change in consumption patterns


It is necessary to use resources more efficiently and sustainable practice must be enforced on both institutional and individual level to be effective


Our solution

We aim to solve the issue of food waste and ensuring a more sustainable future for the younger generations by putting the idea of “from farm to table” into practice. We get the “ugly” produce directly from farmers and pack it together with relevant recipes and information of where it came from. These boxes are sent to schools that have partnered with us and then used in their food and health teaching. This way students learn not only that vegetables come in a lot of different shapes and sizes, but they also get to enjoy food from local farms and see in practice what a sustainable future looks like.
